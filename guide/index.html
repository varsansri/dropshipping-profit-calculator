Is Dropshipping Profitable in 2024? True Margin Analysis

Yes — dropshipping is still profitable in 2024. But the margins are thinner than most gurus advertise, and the math looks very different when you include every real cost. Here's the honest breakdown.

The True Cost Breakdown on Every Order

Selling Price: $49.99 Product cost (supplier): -$12.00 Shopify fee (2%): -$1.00 Payment processor (3%): -$1.50 Facebook Ads (CPA): -$18.00 Return rate (5%): -$2.50 Chargeback buffer (1%): -$0.50 ───────────────────────── TRUE PROFIT: $14.49 (29% margin)

Without factoring in ads and returns, this product looks like a 74% margin. With everything included, it's 29%. This is why so many beginners think they're making money and then wonder why their bank account isn't growing.

What Margin Do You Actually Need?

  • Under 20% net margin: Unsustainable. One bad ad week erases profit.
  • 20–30% net margin: Viable but tight. Requires efficient ad spend.
  • 30–50% net margin: Healthy. Good room for scaling and testing.
  • 50%+ net margin: Excellent. Usually means private label or very high AOV products.

The #1 Mistake Beginners Make

Calculating profit without including ad spend. If your Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) from Facebook/TikTok ads is $25 and your product profit before ads is $20, you are losing $5 per sale — not making $20. Always subtract your real CPA (cost per acquisition) when calculating margin.

Reality check: Average Facebook/TikTok ad CPA for dropshipping in 2024 ranges from $15–$45 per order depending on niche. Products with under $30 profit before ads are very hard to make work profitably with paid traffic.

Products That Still Work in 2024

  • High-AOV products ($80+) where a $25 CPA leaves healthy margin
  • Consumables with strong repeat purchase rates
  • Problem-solving products with emotional buyers (pain relief, pet, beauty)
  • Seasonal/trending products with organic TikTok virality potential
  • Niche items hard to find locally but in demand online
Pro tip: Calculate your break-even CPA before running a single ad. Break-even CPA = (Selling price - product cost - platform fees - payment fees) × (1 - return rate). This is the maximum you can pay per sale and still break even. If your market's average CPA is above this number, the product won't work.
